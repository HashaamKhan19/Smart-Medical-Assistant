Allergies:

1. Penicillin
   - Reaction: Rash, Hives
   - First Noted: March 2005
   - Severity: Moderate
   - Management:
     - Avoidance of penicillin and related antibiotics
     - Use of alternative antibiotics (e.g., erythromycin, clindamycin)
   - Last Reaction: March 2015
   - Additional Notes: Patient carries an allergy alert card for penicillin.

2. Peanuts
   - Reaction: Anaphylaxis
   - First Noted: June 2008
   - Severity: Severe
   - Management:
     - Strict avoidance of peanuts and peanut-containing products
     - Use of epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) in case of accidental exposure
     - Antihistamines (e.g., diphenhydramine) for mild reactions
   - Last Reaction: January 2012
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to always carry an EpiPen and wear a medical alert bracelet.

3. Dust Mites
   - Reaction: Sneezing, Runny Nose, Itchy Eyes
   - First Noted: September 1995
   - Severity: Mild to Moderate
   - Management:
     - Use of antihistamines (e.g., cetirizine) during peak allergy seasons
     - Regular cleaning and vacuuming of home
     - Use of dust mite-proof covers on pillows and mattresses
   - Last Reaction: Ongoing during high dust periods
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to use air purifiers at home.

4. Pollen (Various Trees and Grasses)
   - Reaction: Sneezing, Runny Nose, Itchy Eyes
   - First Noted: April 1997
   - Severity: Mild to Moderate
   - Management:
     - Use of antihistamines (e.g., cetirizine) during pollen seasons
     - Nasal saline irrigation to relieve symptoms
     - Limiting outdoor activities during high pollen days
   - Last Reaction: Spring 2023
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to follow local pollen forecasts and take preventive measures.

5. Shellfish (Shrimp, Crab, Lobster)
   - Reaction: Hives, Swelling of Lips and Throat
   - First Noted: July 2010
   - Severity: Severe
   - Management:
     - Strict avoidance of shellfish and shellfish-containing products
     - Use of epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) in case of accidental exposure
     - Antihistamines (e.g., diphenhydramine) for mild reactions
   - Last Reaction: July 2015
   - Additional Notes: Patient carries an allergy alert card for shellfish.

6. Latex
   - Reaction: Contact Dermatitis (Redness, Itching, Swelling)
   - First Noted: November 2009
   - Severity: Moderate
   - Management:
     - Avoidance of latex-containing products (e.g., gloves, balloons)
     - Use of latex-free alternatives (e.g., nitrile or vinyl gloves)
   - Last Reaction: February 2020
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to inform healthcare providers about latex allergy before any procedures.

7. Insect Stings (Bees, Wasps)
   - Reaction: Local Swelling, Redness, Itching
   - First Noted: August 1998
   - Severity: Moderate
   - Management:
     - Use of cold compresses and antihistamines (e.g., cetirizine) to reduce symptoms
     - Epinephrine auto-injector (EpiPen) prescribed for potential severe reactions
   - Last Reaction: July 2019
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to avoid areas with high insect activity and wear protective clothing when outdoors.

8. Mold
   - Reaction: Sneezing, Coughing, Itchy Eyes
   - First Noted: May 2002
   - Severity: Mild
   - Management:
     - Use of antihistamines (e.g., cetirizine) during mold exposure
     - Regular cleaning to prevent mold growth at home
     - Use of dehumidifiers to control indoor humidity levels
   - Last Reaction: Ongoing during mold exposure
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to avoid damp areas and ensure proper ventilation at home.

9. Cat Dander
   - Reaction: Sneezing, Runny Nose, Itchy Eyes
   - First Noted: February 2001
   - Severity: Mild to Moderate
   - Management:
     - Use of antihistamines (e.g., cetirizine) during exposure to cats
     - Avoidance of prolonged exposure to cats and cat dander
     - Regular cleaning and vacuuming to reduce dander at home
   - Last Reaction: Ongoing during exposure to cats
   - Additional Notes: Patient advised to minimize contact with cats and use air purifiers to reduce dander.

10. Aspirin
    - Reaction: Hives, Stomach Pain
    - First Noted: October 2003
    - Severity: Moderate
    - Management:
      - Avoidance of aspirin and aspirin-containing medications
      - Use of alternative pain relievers (e.g., acetaminophen)
    - Last Reaction: October 2008
    - Additional Notes: Patient advised to inform healthcare providers about aspirin allergy before any treatments.
